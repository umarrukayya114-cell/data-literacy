# Practice Exercises

Use these exercises to practice the concepts discussed in class.
Try to answer them **before** looking up notes.

---

## Practice 1: Types of Data

Identify the data type for each example.

1. Favorite movie genre  
2. Number of emails received in a day  
3. Satisfaction level (low, medium, high)  
4. Time spent studying (hours)  
5. Country of residence  

---

## Practice 2: Distribution Shapes

For each situation, predict the most likely distribution shape.

1. Household income  
2. Scores on a very difficult exam  
3. Heights of adult men  
4. Number of children per household  

Choose from:
- Symmetric
- Right-skewed
- Left-skewed
- Uniform
- Bimodal

---

## Practice 3: Interpreting Distributions

Answer in one or two sentences.

1. What does it mean if a distribution is right-skewed?  
2. Why might income data not be symmetric?  
3. What could cause a bimodal distribution in real data?
